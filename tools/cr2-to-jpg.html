Understanding Canon CR2 Format

CR2 (Canon RAW 2nd edition) is Canon's proprietary RAW format used by all Canon EOS DSLR and mirrorless cameras. CR2 files are essentially digital negatives—they contain all the unprocessed data captured by Canon's sensor without any in-camera processing, compression, or adjustments. This means CR2 files record every photon of light that hits the sensor, preserving maximum image information including wider dynamic range (14-bit), greater color depth, and more details in highlights and shadows.

This unprocessed nature gives Canon photographers maximum editing flexibility. You can adjust exposure, white balance, contrast, and colors extensively without degrading image quality, and recover details from overexposed highlights or underexposed shadows that would be permanently lost in JPG. However, CR2 files require specialized software (Canon Digital Photo Professional, Adobe Lightroom, Photoshop) to view and edit, and their large file sizes make storage and sharing impractical without conversion to more manageable formats like JPG.
