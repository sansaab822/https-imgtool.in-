Why Convert TIFF to JPG?

  • Massive File Size Reduction: TIFF files can be 10-100 times larger than JPG. Converting to JPG saves significant storage space and enables faster file transfers.
  • Web Optimization: JPG is the standard format for web images, ensuring fast loading times and better user experience on websites and blogs.
  • Email Compatibility: Most email services have attachment size limits. JPG files are small enough to send without compression or file-sharing services.
  • Universal Compatibility: JPG is supported by virtually every device, application, and platform, while TIFF requires specialized software.
  • Social Media Ready: All social media platforms accept JPG, but many don't support TIFF format for uploads.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does converting from TIFF to JPG reduce image quality?
Yes, JPG uses lossy compression, so some image data is discarded. However, our tool is optimized to maintain high quality while significantly reducing file size. For photographs, the difference is often imperceptible at quality settings above 75%.
Why are TIFF files so much larger than JPG?
TIFF uses lossless compression to preserve every detail of the image, resulting in files that can be 10-100 times larger than JPG. This makes TIFF ideal for professional archiving and printing but impractical for web use or sharing.
